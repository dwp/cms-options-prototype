The Child Maintenance Service can:

  • work out how much child maintenance you should pay and when you should pay it 
  • take action if payments are not made in full or on time
  • help find the other parent if you have lost contact

How you get child maintenance

There are two child maintenance service types:

Direct pay - You will pay child maintenance into the other parent's bank or building society. You manage payments using an online account. 

Collect and Pay - the Child Maintenance Service will take payment from you and pass it on to the other parent. There are fees for both parents if you use this service. 

You do not have to have any contact with the other parent for either service type.

To decide your service type, we take into account:

  • both parent's service type preference
  • your payment history using the Child Maintenance Service (if you have any) 

How long an application takes

It can take up to 6 weeks to process an application and arrange a payment plan. Complex cases may take longer.

It will take longer to process if your child is not registered for Child Benefit (opens in new tab).
