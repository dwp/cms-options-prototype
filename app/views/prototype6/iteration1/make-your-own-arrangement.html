One of your options: make a private arrangement 

You can make a private arrangement with your child’s other parent. No one else has to be involved. You and the other parent can organise everything yourselves.

For example, you and the other parent can agree:

Making your own arrangement might be a good option if:

  • you and the other parent can communicate well
  • you feel safe dealing with the other parent
  • you think the other parent will stick to an agreement
The Child Maintenance Service cannot enforce or take legal action to recover any missed maintenance payments from a private arrangement.
Is there support available if I make a private arrangement with the other parent??
There are online resources that can help you and the other parent discuss, calculate and record a private child maintenance arrangement. These resources are shown in the next steps of this service, if you choose to make a private arrangement.
