How you pay child maintenance

The Child Maintenance Service has two service types. After you submit your application, we will tell you which service type you’ll use and how much child maintenance you should pay.  

Direct Pay

Direct Pay is a free service that you manage using your online child maintenance account.

You will need the other parent’s bank or building society details to make payments to them. If you do not want to have any contact with the other parent, we can get their details for you. 

If you miss a payment or do not pay in full and on time, the other parent can:

  • report it in their online account 
  • request to change to the Collect and Pay service

Collect and Pay

The Child Maintenance Service will:

  • collect payment from you and pass it to the other parent
  • manage payments for you
  • take action if you miss a payment

There are extra fees for this service:

  • 20% is added to the amount you pay
  • 4% is taken off the amount the other parent gets

For example, if the payment amount was £100, you would pay £120 and the other parent would get £96.
